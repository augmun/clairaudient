What Is Clairaudient?

Clairaudience is the psychic ability to hear. Its definition is derived from the French-Latin origins clair, which means "clear," and audience, which is derived from the basic word audientia, which implies hearing or listening. Overall, it signifies "clear hearing."

When clients come in for psychic examinations and tell about their clairaudient experiences, they are frequently the most terrifying. This is because, as a culture, we are often terrified of what we cannot see or comprehend in the physical domain.

Clients who come in and talk about their experiences are equally terrified since psychic hearing is frequently labelled as an auditory hallucination, and people are given diagnoses like psychosis or schizophrenia and made to feel like they are "wrong." Now, here's the thing: if a person has too many energies in their field that are not their own (an article on this will be published in the coming months), and those energies carry sounds and voices with them, it can actually affect and alter a person's brain chemistry, causing them to present with misfiring synapses that resemble schizophrenia.

This is why, no matter what psychic powers you have (and everyone does... it's human nature to have psychic ability), it's always crucial to determine whether or not what you're feeling is "yours." If you hear sounds, whispers, or voices, inquire as to who is making them. You could get a response. Sometimes a voice belongs to you; it is the voice of your Higher Self or intuition. It is critical to pause for a moment and ask if what you are hearing is yours or someone else's, and if it is someone else's, then inquire as to whose. It's possible you won't get an answer, and that's fine.

People do not simply experience clairaudience through voices. Some people have clairaudience and experience it by hearing melodies in their brains, high pitched tones or ringing, deep hums, sounds of equipment like washing machines or automobile motors, or nature noises like rain, wind, or fire crackling.

As a result, the vast majority of persons who demonstrate clairaudience are unaware that they are doing so. People go about their regular lives, hearing pitches or tunes in their heads, and they don't think anything of it. Furthermore, many people who are aware of their clairaudience are unable to understand the messages that are being sent to them. "I keep hearing this pitch!" remark so many of my customers. "What exactly does this pitch mean?"

Symptoms of clairaudience include an increased sensitivity to loud noises in the physical domain, a preference for music, and the appearance of being physically deaf.

Clairaudient Spiritual

Clairaudient Spirituals & Science

Spiritualism is a religious movement that holds that the spirits of the departed continue to exist beyond physical death.

Mediums claim to be able to interact with spirits by seeing ("clairvoyant"), feeling ("clairsentient"), or hearing ("clairaudient").

Religious and spiritual experiences (RSEs), such as clairaudience, have been compared to auditory hallucinations in patients with specific mental health problems, according to research from the Universities of Cardiff, Northampton, and Lancaster in the United Kingdom.

The 'clairaudient' encounter

A recent research published in the journal Mental Health, Religion, and Culture investigated the relationships between auditory spiritual communications experienced by mediums, beliefs, and personality.

The research was conducted as part of the Hearing the Voice initiative. The study was conducted by Dr. Adam J. Powell of the Department of Theology and Religion at the University of Durham in the United Kingdom and Dr. Peter Moseley of the Department of Psychology at Northumbria University in the United Kingdom.

"Spiritualists tend to have uncommon auditory experiences that are good, begin early in life, and are typically controllable," notes Dr. Moseley. "Understanding how they emerge is critical since it may help us understand more about painful or uncontrollable experiences with hearing voices."

How to Evaluate a Clairaudient Experience

The researchers enlisted the help of 65 spiritualists as well as 143 additional people to serve as a general population control group. The majority came from the United Kingdom, North America, Australasia, and Europe.

The researchers requested the participants to complete customised versions of online questionnaires that are known to reliably measure various qualities.

These questionnaires were as follows:

The Tellegen Absorption Scale employs yes/no questions to assess a person's proclivity to become totally absorbed in internal and external stimuli such as movies, mental pictures, music, or ideas. This is also known as absorption proneness.
The Revised Launay-Slade Hallucination Scale evaluates a participant's proclivity for auditory and visual hallucinations.
The Revised Paranormal Beliefs Scale evaluates participants' perceptions of traditional religious beliefs, psi (extrasensory perception and the ability to influence physical entities without interaction), witchcraft, superstition, extraordinary life forms, precognition (the ability to predict the future), and Spiritualism.
The Aspects of Identity Questionnaire IV examines the relevance of personal identity, relational identity, social identity, and communal identity as perceived by the participants.
Participants in the spiritualist group also filled out a questionnaire detailing how frequently, in what situations, and for how long their experiences occurred.

Frequency, context, and place

The average age of spiritualist participants when they first had a clairaudient experience was 21.7 years, however most persons had it before the age of 20 or around the age of 40.

As many as 44.6 percent of respondents indicated they experienced clairaudient experiences on a regular basis, and 33.8 percent said they had such an event the day before.

In terms of where these experiences occurred, 79 percent of individuals reported having them both in spiritualist contexts (such as a church) and outside of such settings.

A total of 12.9 percent of individuals reported having clairaudience solely outside of spiritualist situations, while another 8.1 percent reported having clairaudience only within spiritualist contexts.

According to the reports, 65.1 percent of clairaudient experiences happened inside the medium's mind, 31.7 percent manifested both internally and outside, and 3.2 percent manifested exclusively externally.

In terms of the initial clairaudient experience:

44.8 percent of the participants reported having had clairaudience prior to experiencing Spiritualism.
Spiritualism was mentioned by 29.3 percent of the individuals before they experienced clairaudience.
25.9 percent of all participants indicated their first experience with clairaudience coincided with their first interaction with Spiritualism.

What does this mean?

Spiritualists were more susceptible to absorption and both spiritual and non-spiritual auditory hallucination than the general population.

Furthermore, the higher the frequency of the clairaudient experience, the greater the proclivity for absorption within the Spiritualist community.

The study reveals a link between spiritual beliefs and absorption in the general population. The relationship between spiritual beliefs and hallucination-proneness was not significant in this group.

The study also corroborated prior results that people are more likely to become interested in the paranormal as a result of an uncommon sensory encounter, rather than the other way around.

Surprisingly, the researchers discovered that spiritualists were no more reliant on the opinions and viewpoints of others to develop their sense of self than the general population. In fact, the spiritualist group outperformed the control group in terms of personal identification.

The spiritualist group members had much less years of formal schooling. However, because no questionnaire measures showed a significant connection with schooling, this factor cannot explain the disparities between the two groups.

Can mediums actually communicate with the dead?

This is a centuries-old subject, but the study did not investigate the veracity of spiritual experiences. It instead compared the auditory hallucinations, beliefs, and identity features of mediums to those of the general population.

Because the study relied on self-reports, it is unclear if any of the connections are the consequence of the technique itself. Participants, for example, may not have completely appreciated the distinction between similarly phrased items on the four scales and hence selected comparable replies.

More study is also needed to determine if absorption predisposes people to RSEs or makes them believe that RSEs are possible.

Another complicated link that research has yet to investigate is the effect of diverse cultures, ideologies, and belief systems in absorption and RSEs.
